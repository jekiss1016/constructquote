MyBidBook User Guide

Your step-by-step guide to managing estimates, catalogs, margins, and customer databases.

Mobile Optimization: MyBidBook is fully optimized for smartphones and tablets. On small screens, the side navigation collapses into a sliding drawer. Tap the hamburger menu icon (☰) at the top left of the screen to open the menu. Large lists (like Quotes, Customers, and Dashboard alerts) automatically format as vertical cards so you can see all your data without scrolling horizontally, and you can tap anywhere on a card to open or edit the record directly.

Dashboard Guide

The Dashboard is the home view of MyBidBook. It aggregates real-time indicators and tracks your sales performance.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Active Quotes: The count and cumulative total dollar value of all proposals currently in Pending or Under Review status.
  • Win Rate: The percentage of finalized proposals won by your company, calculated against total won/lost outcomes.
  • Won Proposals (Month): Total dollar value of proposals marked as Won within the current calendar month.

Alerts Panel

This panel warns you about quotes nearing their expiration date or already expired, enabling quick client follow-ups. You can customize the threshold of days to show using the dropdown in the panel header (from 10 up to 90 days, defaulting to 10 days).

Pro-Tip: Clicking the "View" or "Edit" shortcut links inside the Alerts panel takes you directly to the quote editor or preview page for that estimate.

Quotes Manager

Use the Quotes Manager to locate, duplicate, filter, and archive project bids.

Workflow Actions

  • Status Tabs: Instantly filter quotes by their status: Pending, Under Review, Won, Lost, or Draft.
  • Search Bar: Search by project ID, company name, contact person, or scope details.
  • Duplicate/Template: Clone an existing quote to build a new one using the same project scope and item layout without manual entries.
  • Archiving/Deleting: Delete invalid proposals using the bin icon to keep your active list clean.

Quote Builder

The Builder is the engine of MyBidBook. This page is where you configure bid sections, assign products, material values, labor costs, and apply profit margins.

Client Details

  • Select Customer (Required): You must select an existing customer record from the dropdown. Manual name inputs have been disabled to ensure consistency with your database.
  • Project Address (Optional): Split into distinct inputs for Street Address, City, State, and Zip Code (with 5-digit number verification) for cleaner formatting.
  • Quote Expiration: Restricts selection to at least Creation Date + 1 Day, which is automatically enforced.

Section Management & Price Summary

  • Sections: Quotes can be broken down into structured sections (e.g., "Phase 1: Foundation", "Phase 2: Framing").
  • Quote Configuration Panel: Houses your Markup %, Sales Tax %, and Tax exemption settings, alongside print layout controls.
  • Price Summary Breakdown: Displays Material, Labor, and Markup subtotals under a Combined Subtotal, followed by calculated Tax and the final Total Price.

Warning: Ensure the project has a valid "Created Date" specified before saving, otherwise the quote will not be written to the database.

Quote Preview

This page gives you a clean representation of the quote proposal as your client will view it. It is print-friendly and supports several customizable display settings.

Print Settings Panel

  • Hide/Show Material Costs: Suppress raw costs to protect your wholesale rates.
  • Hide/Show Quantities & UOM: Show only scope items without detailing precise unit calculations.
  • Hide/Show Section Breakdown: Simplify the layout to present a single global quote total.

Client Actions

Mark quotes as Won or Lost directly from this page, print directly to paper, or email the quote directly to the customer. You can also view a history of all sent emails, including recipient lists and quote versions, under the Email Correspondence log box in the sidebar.

Product Catalog

The Catalog stores standard products, materials, units of measure (UOM), and standard wholesale base costs.

Default Categories

When starting your company database, MyBidBook initializes a default category called "Category 1". You can rename this category at any time to match your industry (e.g. "Electrical" or "Concrete") as long as you do not have active products attached to a quote under that category.

Customer Manager

The Customer Manager houses details for clients, developers, and project sites.

Proposals History

When inspecting a customer record, a table at the bottom will automatically list all historical and active proposals linked to that client, along with status badges and pricing summaries.

Customer Email Body Defaults

On each customer record, you can set a Customer Quote Email Body Default template. This is pre-populated with the company-wide default template, but can be customized for specific clients. When emailing a quote to the customer, this client-specific template will automatically prefill the email body, allowing you to customize it further before sending.

Settings & Security

Configure company properties, logo graphics, backups, and user security policies.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

To secure your account, you can enable MFA (TOTP) from the Settings page. This will generate a QR code for your authenticator app (such as Google Authenticator or Duo) to request a secure 6-digit verification code at login.

Local Database Backups

Under the settings view, you can export a full backup of all products, categories, customers, and quote configurations as a single, compressed JSON file. This can be re-imported later if you move devices or want to restore a past project archive.

Customer Quote Email Default Templates

Under Company Settings, you can configure a global Customer Quote Email Body Default template. This body text defaults down to any new or existing customer record, ensuring a consistent brand voice when sending quotes.

PWA & Offline Support

MyBidBook supports Progressive Web App (PWA) installation for native-like desktop and mobile experiences, as well as offline connection handling.

PWA Installation

To install the app on your device, click the Get The App button located in the sidebar navigation:

  • Windows & Android: Tapping the button will trigger the browser's native PWA installation dialog. Once accepted, the app will run in its own standalone window.
  • iOS (iPhone/iPad): iOS requires Safari for app installation. Clicking the button will take you to a guided installation helper page. From Safari, tap the Share icon and select Add to Home Screen.

Offline Mode & Connection Warnings

If your device loses internet connectivity, a red warning banner will appear at the top of the screen: "You are offline. Some functions are not available. Please try again when connected to the internet."

While offline, you can continue browsing cached data and local catalog items. However, database synchronizations, quote updates, and photo uploads will be paused until a stable internet connection is restored.
